In praise of small language models

Large language models (LLMs) are progressing at breakneck speed, captivating investors and sweeping through markets. However, their tendency to hallucinate and their inherent opacity limit their utility for investment decisions. Small language models (SLMs), in contrast, offer reliability and interpretability. These qualities make SLMs especially well-suited for answering highly specific questions, as this paper demonstrates using a case study on China's housing market slowdown.
